# Claims

- Mechanism claim tested: calibrated precontact shear, friction prior, compliance estimate, visual gap, uncertainty, and latency margin can warn about future micro-slip before stable contact.
- Evidence claim supported: the v5 CPU-only benchmark is reproducible and includes strong local baselines, seed-level paired tests, ablations, stress sweeps, fixed-risk checks, and negative cases.
- Evidence claim not supported: `calibrated_precontact_microslip_v5` is not stronger than the best particle-friction or recovery-aware MPC baselines on frozen hard-aggregate AP, success, gross slip, robust utility, burden, stress, or fixed-risk gates.
- Scope claim: the artifact supports a reproducible negative archive, not real-robot deployment or ICLR-main submission.
- Unsupported claims explicitly avoided: no claim of SOTA robot performance, no claim of hardware validation, and no claim that precontact micro-slip causality is identified by the local score alone.
